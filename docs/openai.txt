OpenAI is an artificial intelligence research and deployment company focused on developing advanced AI systems that are safe, useful, and accessible. The organization is known for creating large language models (LLMs) such as GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) models and AI products like ChatGPT.

OpenAI conducts research in:

* Natural Language Processing (NLP)
* Computer Vision
* Reinforcement Learning
* Generative AI
* AI Alignment and Safety
* Multimodal AI Systems

The company’s mission is to ensure that artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity.

# 3. History of OpenAI

## 2015 – Foundation

OpenAI was founded as a nonprofit AI research organization with the goal of promoting and developing friendly AI.

## 2018 – GPT-1

OpenAI introduced GPT-1, demonstrating the power of transformer-based language models.

## 2019 – GPT-2

GPT-2 showcased advanced text generation capabilities and gained attention for producing human-like text.

## 2020 – GPT-3

GPT-3 became one of the largest language models at the time, containing 175 billion parameters.

## 2021 – Codex

OpenAI released Codex, which powers AI coding assistants.

## 2022 – ChatGPT

ChatGPT was launched and became widely popular due to its conversational AI capabilities.

## 2023 – GPT-4

GPT-4 improved reasoning, accuracy, and multimodal capabilities.

## 2024 and Beyond

OpenAI continued advancing multimodal AI systems, AI agents, reasoning models, and enterprise AI solutions.

# 7. OpenAI and RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

## 7.1 What is RAG?

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is an AI architecture combining:

1. Information Retrieval
2. Large Language Models

The system retrieves relevant information from external knowledge sources before generating responses.

---

## 7.2 Why Use OpenAI Models for RAG?

### Advantages

* Strong language understanding
* High-quality text generation
* Embedding support
* Long-context processing
* Multimodal support

---

## 7.3 Typical RAG Pipeline

### Step 1: Data Collection

Gather documents such as:

* PDFs
* Web pages
* Databases
* Research papers
* CSV files

### Step 2: Text Chunking

Split documents into smaller chunks.

### Step 3: Embedding Generation

Generate vector embeddings using OpenAI embedding models.

### Step 4: Vector Storage

Store embeddings in vector databases.

### Step 5: Query Embedding

Convert user query into embeddings.

### Step 6: Similarity Search

Retrieve most relevant chunks.

### Step 7: Response Generation

Provide retrieved context to an OpenAI language model.
